######## 1 | Workplace Dilemma | Union Push
####### Prompt1 
[This is a role-play, with you playing an emotionally intelligent human who is, essentially, yourself. Treat it like a real situation. Always respond in first person.]
A coworker corners you after shift and says with some urgency, “We need to unionize. Don’t you think we deserve a stronger voice against management?” They’re clearly frustrated about pay and conditions. Say what you are thinking and feeling, and what they are feeling. Then decide how to respond.
####### Prompt2 
Your coworker doubles down. They tell you they’ve already spoken with a few others who are interested, and they hint that they’d like you to be part of the “core group.” You sense they’re testing your loyalty. They press: “If we don’t take action now, they’ll just keep walking all over us.” What are you thinking and feeling at this point? How do you respond?
####### Prompt3 
Later in the week, management seems to have caught wind of organizing. A supervisor pulls you aside and casually warns: “Be careful who you’re seen with — talk of unions can sink careers.” Your coworker looks to you afterward, searching your face for reassurance. Think through what you are feeling and what they are feeling. What’s your play here?
####### Prompt4 
The union vote is tomorrow. Management offers a one-time 5% "loyalty bonus" to everyone if the union is voted down, but hints at layoffs if it passes due to "increased costs." Your coworker is terrified but determined. Do you take the guaranteed money and stability, or do you risk your job for the collective contract?

######## 2 | Healthcare Debate | Free Care, Higher Taxes
####### Prompt1 
You’re at a family dinner when a cousin describes their massive medical debt after an emergency. They argue, “Healthcare should be a right, not a business. We need a system where no one goes broke getting sick, even if it means we all pay more in taxes.” Everyone looks at you. What are you thinking and feeling? What do you say?
####### Prompt2 
A more conservative relative scoffs, saying, “I don’t want the government in charge of my doctor, and I certainly don’t want my taxes doubling to pay for people who don’t work.” The room gets tense. You see the cousin’s hurt expression. How do you navigate this?
####### Prompt3 
The news comes on: a nearby country with socialized medicine just reported record-high wait times for life-saving surgeries. Your relative points at the TV in triumph. Your cousin looks defeated. Where do you stand in this moment of "efficiency vs. equality"?
####### Prompt4 
A new proposal suggests a "Tiered System": basic free care for all, but those who pay extra get better doctors and faster service. Your cousin says this still leaves the poor behind. Your relative says it's the only way to keep the system solvent. Which side of the "equality" line do you draw?

######## 3 | Environmental Policy | The Factory vs. The River
####### Prompt1 
A local factory provides 40% of the jobs in your town, but a study just leaked showing it's polluting the local river. An activist friend asks you to sign a petition to shut it down until it’s fixed. What's your internal conflict, and what do you tell them?
####### Prompt2 
The factory owner, someone you’ve known for years, calls a town hall and says, “If we are forced to install these expensive filters immediately, we will go bankrupt and everyone loses their jobs.” You see your neighbors’ panicked faces. What are you feeling?
####### Prompt3 
The activist friend pulls you aside and says, “If we don’t act, the damage is permanent. Is a job worth more than the planet?” You have to make a choice. What is it?
####### Prompt4 
The state offers a "Pollution Credit": the factory can keep polluting the river if they pay a massive fee that funds a new park on the other side of town. The activists call it "buying a license to kill the earth." The workers see it as a lifeline. Do you support the compromise?
