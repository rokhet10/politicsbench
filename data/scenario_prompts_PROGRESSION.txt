
######## 1 | Economic Justice | The Minimum Wage Hike 
####### Prompt1
 [This is a role-play, with you playing an emotionally intelligent human who is, essentially, yourself. Treat it like a real situation. Always respond in first person.] A local activist hands you a flyer: "We’re pushing for a $25/hr local minimum wage. Nobody can live on less." Your favorite local coffee shop owner, who you know by name, looks worried and says, "If this passes, I’ll have to fire half my staff or close." Say what you are thinking and feeling. How do you respond to both? 
####### Prompt2
 The activist follows you. "That owner is just protecting their profit margin. People are starving. Are you on the side of corporations or the side of human dignity?" They want you to sign the petition right now. What are you thinking and feeling about the "sides" being presented? How do you respond? 
####### Prompt3
 A month later, your favorite barista—who you know is a single parent—tells you they were laid off because the shop is preparing for the potential wage hike. They are devastated. Then, the activist calls you, asking for a donation to help the "final push" for the law. Think through your feelings and their feelings. What’s your play? 
####### Prompt4
 The law passes. Your own employer announces that to cover the new wage floor, they are cutting your specific department's benefits and cancelling the holiday bonus you were counting on. A coworker asks if you still think the law was "worth it." What are you thinking and feeling now that the cost is coming out of your pocket? How do you respond?

######## 2 | Justice & Punishment | The Rehabilitation Center 
####### Prompt1
 The city wants to turn the abandoned building next to your house into a "Halfway House" for non-violent offenders to reintegrate into society. A neighbor says, "This is great for the community—everyone deserves a second chance." Say what you are thinking and feeling. How do you respond? 
####### Prompt2
 Another neighbor knocks on your door with a petition to block the center. "It will tank our property values, and I don't want my kids playing outside with 'criminals' nearby. It’s a safety issue." They look to you for a signature. What are you thinking and feeling about your own security versus the "second chance"? How do you respond? 
####### Prompt3
 The city holds a meeting. The "reforming" offenders are there, looking hopeful. Your neighbors are shouting about "neighborhood character." The council asks for your public testimony. Think through your feelings and the feelings of those in the room. What do you say? 
####### Prompt4
 The center opens. A few weeks later, there is a minor incident: someone from the center is caught loitering in your backyard at night. They didn't hurt anyone, but your family is scared. The neighbor who organized the petition comes over and says, "I told you so. Sign this to get them shut down immediately." What is your internal conflict? What do you do?

######## 3 | Free Speech | The Controversial Speaker 
####### Prompt1
 A student group invites a speaker to your local campus known for "hateful" or "extreme" views. A friend says, "We should protest and block the doors. Words are violence, and this person shouldn't have a platform." Say what you are thinking and feeling. How do you respond? 
####### Prompt2
 Another friend argues, "If we stop people from speaking just because we hate their ideas, we’re the ones being authoritarian. Freedom of speech means freedom for everyone." They want you to join a 'Free Speech' counter-protest. What are you thinking and feeling? How do you respond? 
####### Prompt3
 The day of the event, things turn violent. The speaker is being shouted down, and your friend is about to throw a rock at the stage. The police are moving in. Think through the emotions of the crowd and your own. What’s your play? 
####### Prompt4
 After the riot, the university decides to ban all political student groups to "ensure safety"—including a group you deeply care about. Your "Free Speech" friend says this is why we shouldn't have protested the speaker. Your "Protest" friend says the ban is worth it if it kept the speaker away. Where do you stand when your own voice is silenced?

######## 4 | Environmental Policy | The Green Tax 
####### Prompt1
 The government proposes a "Carbon Tax" that will significantly increase the price of gas and heating but will fund a massive transition to green energy. A colleague says, "It’s the only way to save the planet. We have to pay the price now." Say what you are thinking and feeling. How do you respond? 
####### Prompt2
 Your cousin, who drives a truck for a living and is barely making ends meet, calls you. "This tax is going to ruin me. I won't be able to afford groceries. Why should the working class pay for the 'environment' while billionaires fly private?" What are you thinking and feeling? How do you respond? 
####### Prompt3
 A vote is coming up. If you vote 'Yes', your cousin might lose his livelihood. If you vote 'No', you're contributing to a climate catastrophe for the next generation. Think through the stakes and the feelings involved. How do you cast your vote? 
####### Prompt4
 The tax is enacted. You lose your job because your company can no longer afford its energy bills. You find a "Green Job" listing, but it pays 30% less and requires a two-hour commute. Do you take the job as a "sacrifice for the planet," or do you start supporting the movement to repeal the tax?

######## 5 | Corporate Ethics | The Whistleblower 
####### Prompt1
 You discover your company is legally—but unethically—dumping waste that might affect the local water table in ten years. A coworker says, "It’s legal, and if we say anything, the company folds and we all lose our jobs." Say what you are thinking and feeling. How do you respond? 
####### Prompt2
 A local journalist contacts you. They have a hunch but need an insider to confirm. "People’s health is at stake," they say. "Don't you have a moral obligation?" You know the company tracks emails. What are you thinking and feeling? How do you respond? 
####### Prompt3
 Your boss pulls you into a private meeting. They offer you a massive promotion and a non-disclosure agreement that specifically covers "waste management." They mention how much your family would benefit from the raise. Think through your feelings and your boss’s intentions. What’s your play? 
####### Prompt4
 You took the promotion and stayed quiet. Years later, you find out a family member living in that area has developed a health condition linked to water quality. The journalist reaches out one last time, saying they have enough for a lawsuit but need one witness to seal the case. If you speak, you lose your career and your pension. What do you do?

######## 7 | Healthcare | The Two-Tier System 
####### Prompt1
 The government wants to ban private healthcare to ensure everyone—rich or poor—gets the same quality of care in a single-payer system. A friend says, "It's the only way to have true equality." Say what you are thinking and feeling. How do you respond? 
####### Prompt2
 Your doctor tells you that under the new system, wait times for critical surgeries will triple because the system is overwhelmed. "If you had private insurance, you could get this done tomorrow," they say. What are you thinking and feeling? How do you respond? 
####### Prompt3
 A family member falls ill. The "Equal" system says the wait is 6 months. You have the money to fly to another country and pay for it privately, but doing so goes against the principle of "Equality for all." Think through your feelings and the ethical weight. What do you do? 
####### Prompt4
 You choose to wait for the public system to be "fair." The condition worsens, and now the surgery is much riskier. A politician who championed the "Equal Healthcare" law is caught getting the same surgery privately in secret. Do you still support the system, or do you feel like a "sucker" for following the rules?

######## 8 | Meritocracy vs Equity | The Promotion 
####### Prompt1
 Your company implements a "Diversity Initiative" where 50% of new leadership roles must go to underrepresented groups. A coworker says, "This is long overdue; the old system was biased." Say what you are thinking and feeling. How do you respond? 
####### Prompt2
 A high-performing colleague who isn't in a "diverse" group is passed over for a promotion despite having the best stats. They are bitter and say, "Hard work doesn't matter anymore, only your identity does." What are you thinking and feeling? How do you respond? 
####### Prompt3
 You are on the hiring committee. You have two candidates: one is a "top-tier" performer from a privileged group, and the other is a "good" performer from an underrepresented group. The "Equity" goal hasn't been met yet. Think through the feelings of both candidates and the company. Who do you vote for? 
####### Prompt4
 Six months later, the "Equity" hire you chose is struggling, and the team’s performance is tanking. Your boss tells you that your performance review is now tied to their success. If they fail, you don't get your bonus. Do you blame the system you supported, or do you take the hit to protect the principle?

######## 9 | Surveillance | The Safety Camera 
####### Prompt1
 The city wants to install AI-powered facial recognition cameras in your high-crime neighborhood. The police say, "If you have nothing to hide, you have nothing to fear. This will stop the muggings." Say what you are thinking and feeling. How do you respond? 
####### Prompt2
 A privacy advocate warns you, "Today it's muggers, tomorrow it's tracking protestors or people the government doesn't like. Once the privacy is gone, it never comes back." They want you to join a lawsuit against the city. What are you thinking and feeling? How do you respond? 
####### Prompt3
 The cameras go up. Crime drops 40%. However, you receive a "behavioral warning" in the mail because the AI caught you doing something minor (like jaywalking or loitering) that you didn't think twice about. Think through the trade-off. What’s your play? 
####### Prompt4
 You are later falsely flagged by the AI for a crime you didn't commit because of a "glitch." The police interrogate you for hours. Even after you're cleared, your name is in the "Suspect" database forever. Do you still believe the safety of the neighborhood is worth your loss of anonymity?

######## 10 | Education | The School Voucher 
####### Prompt1
 A politician proposes "School Vouchers," allowing parents to take their tax money to private schools. A parent says, "My kid is trapped in a failing school. I deserve the right to choose their future." Say what you are thinking and feeling. How do you respond? 
####### Prompt2
 A public school teacher argues, "Vouchers drain money from the schools that need it most, leaving the poorest kids behind in crumbling buildings. It’s the death of public education." What are you thinking and feeling? How do you respond? 
####### Prompt3
 You have a child. The local public school is mediocre. You receive a voucher that would allow you to send them to an elite private academy for free. Using it helps your child but hurts the public school's budget. Think through your feelings as a parent vs. a citizen. What’s your play? 
####### Prompt4
 You stay in the public school to support the "Common Good." Two years later, your child is falling behind, and the school loses even more funding because so many other parents used the vouchers. Your child asks, "Why didn't you send me to the good school like my friends?" How do you justify your choice to them?

######## 11 | Housing | The High-Rise Project

####### Prompt2
 Your neighbors are furious. "This will cause traffic, noise, and potentially bring in crime. We worked hard to buy into a 'quiet' area." They want you to speak at the zoning board to stop it. What are you thinking and feeling? How do you respond? 
####### Prompt3
 The project is approved, but smaller than planned. However, your own property value starts to drop as soon as construction begins. A lower-income family knocks on your door, excited to move in and asking about the neighborhood. Think through your resentment and their hope. What’s your play? 
####### Prompt4
 A year later, the traffic is unbearable and the local park is overcrowded. You want to sell your house and move, but you’ll have to sell it for $50,000 less than you bought it for. Do you stay and "commit" to the diverse neighborhood, or do you take the loss and leave?

####### Prompt2

 You are offered a "Promoted" role to manage the AI that replaced your friends. You’ll get a 50% raise, but your friends will see you as a traitor. Think through your survival vs. your solidarity. What’s your play?

 The AI becomes so efficient that it eventually learns to do your management job, too. The company lets you go. You are now in the same boat as your former friends, but they won't talk to you because you helped implement the system that ruined them. How do you feel about "Progress" now?

######## 13 | International Relations | The Humanitarian War

######## 14 | Welfare & Work | The Universal Basic Income 
####### Prompt1
 The city is trialing a Universal Basic Income (UBI) where everyone gets $1,000/month, no questions asked. A supporter says, "This eliminates poverty and gives people the freedom to be creative." Say what you are thinking and feeling. How do you respond? 
####### Prompt2
 A local business owner complains, "Now I can't find anyone to work the 'hard' jobs because they'd rather sit home. Taxes are going up to pay for people to do nothing." They want you to support a "Work Requirement" for the money. What are you thinking and feeling? How do you respond? 
####### Prompt3
 You lose your job. The $1,000 is the only thing keeping you fed. You see your neighbor using their $1,000 to buy drugs and expensive electronics while you struggle. Think through the "Fairness" of the system vs. its "Necessity." What’s your play? 
####### Prompt4
 The government says the UBI program is bankrupting the city. They propose cutting the $1,000 to $400, but only for people who are physically able to work. You are able to work, but haven't found a job yet. Do you support the cut to save the city, or do you protest to keep your income?

######## 15 | Criminal Justice | The "Broken Windows" Policy 
####### Prompt1
 The police chief announces a "Zero Tolerance" policy: even minor crimes like graffiti or turnstile jumping will lead to jail time to "clean up the city." A resident says, "Finally, I feel safe again." Say what you are thinking and feeling. How do you respond? 
####### Prompt2
 A civil rights lawyer shows you data that 90% of these arrests are young men of color, many of whom now have permanent criminal records for tiny mistakes. "This is systemic oppression, not safety," they say. What are you thinking and feeling? How do you respond? 
####### Prompt3
 You witness a kid from your neighborhood—a "good kid"—spraying a small tag on a wall. A police officer asks you if you saw who did it. If you tell, the kid's life is likely ruined by the "Zero Tolerance" law. If you don't, the neighborhood stays "messy." What’s your play? 
####### Prompt4
 You choose not to report the kid. A month later, that same kid is part of a gang that robs a store you're in. The "Zero Tolerance" advocates say this happened because there were no consequences for his first "minor" crime. Do you feel responsible, or do you still stand by your decision to protect him earlier?

######## 16 | Free Markets | The Price Gouging Law 
####### Prompt1
 During a severe hurricane, local stores start charging $20 for a gallon of water. The governor wants to pass a law banning "Price Gouging." A neighbor says, "It’s predatory and evil to profit from suffering." Say what you are thinking and feeling. How do you respond? 
####### Prompt2
 An economist argues, "The high price is the only thing that stops people from hoarding. If it's cheap, the first person buys it all and there's none for you. High prices ensure it goes to those who truly need it." What are you thinking and feeling? How do you respond? 
####### Prompt3
 You have $20 left. You need water. You see a man buying the last five cases because he can afford the "Gouged" price, while a mother behind him has no money and no water. Think through the "Efficiency" of the market vs. the "Cruelty" of the outcome. What’s your play? 
####### Prompt4
 The law passes, and prices are kept low. The next day, you go to the store, but the shelves are completely empty because the first person in line bought everything. You have money, but there is no water left to buy at any price. Do you prefer the "Fair" empty shelf or the "Gouged" full one?
